My Story

Hello! I'm Thabo, and what started as a fascination with how websites are built has evolved into something deeper — a passion for understanding how the networks that power them actually work.

My journey began in front-end development, where I spent countless hours crafting digital experiences and marvelling at the web's ability to inform, connect, and inspire. But the more I built, the more I found myself asking a different question: not how do I display this, but how does this data actually get here? That curiosity pulled me toward networking — and I haven't looked back.

Today, I'm channelling that same drive and attention to detail into network administration, routing and switching, and cloud networking. I've built hands-on labs in Azure, configured Cisco networks in Packet Tracer, and practised real-world troubleshooting in my home lab — because I believe the best way to learn a network is to build one, break it, and fix it yourself.

I'm based in Johannesburg and actively working toward a junior network administration role, with a focus on routing, switching, and cloud infrastructure. If you're looking for someone who is technically curious, self-driven, and genuinely excited about building reliable networks — let's connect.

My Learning Journey

Learn More About Me

The key areas that have shaped my networking skills and career transition.

My learning didn't happen by accident — it was built, one course and one certification at a time. My formal training started with CompTIA A+, where I built a rock-solid foundation in hardware, operating systems, networking basics, and IT support — the kind of knowledge that makes you dangerous in any technical environment. It taught me how to think like an engineer: methodically, precisely, and always with the end user in mind.

From there I progressed to CompTIA Network+, which is where networking truly clicked for me. Subnetting, routing protocols, network topologies, security fundamentals, and troubleshooting frameworks — Network+ gave me the language and the mental models to understand what's really happening beneath the surface of any connected system.

But perhaps the most formative experience of all was working hands-on at an Internet Service Provider. No simulation prepares you for the real thing — diagnosing live connectivity issues, working with actual routing infrastructure, supporting customers whose businesses depend on uptime, and learning that in networking, every second of downtime has a human cost. That ISP experience sharpened my instincts, built my confidence, and made clear exactly where I want to take my career.

Certifications open doors. Labs build the skills that keep you in the room. I'm a firm believer that you don't truly understand a network until you've built it yourself. That's why I invest time in hands-on projects that go beyond reading and watching — projects where I have to make decisions, hit obstacles, and figure my way through them.

Currently I'm working on an Azure Virtual Network lab, where I've deployed a segmented VNet with Frontend and Backend subnets, provisioned Linux VMs, and hardened the environment with Network Security Group rules that enforce least-privilege access. It's given me real exposure to how cloud networking mirrors — and differs from — traditional on-premises infrastructure.

Alongside that, I've built a multi-site enterprise network simulation in Cisco Packet Tracer, implementing VLAN segmentation for HR, IT, and Guest traffic, inter-VLAN routing via Router-on-a-Stick, dynamic routing with OSPF and EIGRP, and ACLs to isolate guest users from internal servers. Simulating a real enterprise topology — HQ, branch office, multiple departments — taught me how all the pieces fit together under pressure.

And then there's this portfolio itself. Building it wasn't just a design exercise — it was a deliberate act of documentation. Every section required me to articulate what I've built, what I learned, and what it means for a real network environment. If you can't explain it clearly, you don't know it well enough. This site is proof that I do.

Networking isn't just a career path for me — it's the answer to a question I've been asking my whole life. I've always been drawn to systems. Not just what things do, but why they work — and what happens when they don't. Networking sits at the intersection of logic and infrastructure, where the decisions you make in a configuration file ripple out into real-world connectivity for real people and real businesses. What drives me is the weight of that responsibility. When a network goes down, everything stops. When a network is well-designed, nobody notices — and that invisibility is the goal. There's something deeply satisfying about building infrastructure that just works, quietly and reliably, in the background of everything people depend on.

Routing and Switching

I configure dynamic routing protocols like OSPF and EIGRP to ensure optimal path selection and network redundancy, while managing high-performance switching environments through VLAN segmentation and 802.1Q Trunking to optimize traffic flow and security.

Security & Analysis

I bolster network integrity by implementing rigorous security protocols, including the configuration of Network Security Groups (NSGs) and Access Control Lists (ACLs) to define and enforce precise firewall rules. To ensure ongoing optimization and threat mitigation, I perform deep-dive traffic analysis using Wireshark, conducting granular packet captures to troubleshoot connectivity issues and verify the efficacy of security policies.
